White Chicken Chili Recipe

Description

On a chili fall evening nothing warms my home like a pot of chili! My husband isn't a fan of the traditional tomato based chilies, so in our home we opt in for this healthy white chicken chili inspired by TexMix flavors.

To be honest, I love to cook this recipe even in the warmer monthes. It's a one pot meal that's easy to heat and serve leftovers. Plus with the addition of guac and chips on the side it's a great way to enjoy family meal time.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless chicken breast
  • 1 diced yellow onion
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 24 oz chicken broth
  • 2 15 oz cans great Northern beans drained and rinsed
  • 2 4 oz cans diced green chiles
  • 15 oz can whole kernel corn drained
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 3/4 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp chili powde
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 4 oz cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup half and half

Steps

  1. In a large stock pot, add chopped chicken breast, yellow onion, garlic, and seasonsings.
  2. Top with great Northern beans, green chiles, corn, chicken broth, and cilantro. Stir until mixed.
  3. Cover and cook on medium heat until broth begins to boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Add cream cheese and half and half. Stir, then cover and cook for another 10 minutes, or until chili is creamy and slightly thickened.
  5. Serve with desired toppings. Refrigerate leftovers for up to one week.
